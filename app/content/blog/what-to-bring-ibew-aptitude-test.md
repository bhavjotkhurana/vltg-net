# What to Bring to the IBEW Aptitude Test (and What to Leave Home)

**Short version: bring a photo ID and show up 10 to 15 minutes early. Don't bring a calculator, notes, or references. Pencils and scrap paper are handed to you at the test center. That's really the whole list.**

Test day is stressful enough without wondering whether you packed the right things, so let's keep this simple. The IBEW aptitude test (run by the electrical training ALLIANCE and given through your local JATC) has a short, strict list of what's allowed. (If those names are new to you, here's a [quick guide to who's who](/blog/ibew-neca-njatc-jatc-explained).)

## The one thing you cannot forget: a photo ID

This is the big one. Local training committees are about as blunt as it gets: "No ID, no test. No exceptions!" If you show up without a valid photo ID, you will be turned away and you'll have to reschedule, even if you did everything else right.

So the night before, put your ID somewhere you can't miss it. It's the single item that can end your test day before it starts.

## Show up early, not on time

Plan to arrive 10 to 15 minutes before your scheduled start. Late arrivals aren't seated, and a no-show can come with a rescheduling fee, so give yourself a buffer for parking and finding the room. Treat the start time like it's ten minutes earlier than it is.

## Leave the calculator (and anything like it) at home

You are not allowed to use a calculator on the test, and you shouldn't bring one at all. That includes the sneaky ones: a calculator watch or smartwatch will be set aside during the session. The math is meant to be done by hand, and the center gives you pencils and scrap paper to do it.

If you're wondering how you're supposed to get through 33 algebra questions without one, that's worth reading up on before test day: [here's what the no-calculator rule really means and how to prepare for it](/blog/calculator-ibew-aptitude-test).

## What's provided for you

You don't need to pack a pencil case. The test center supplies:

- Pencils
- Scrap paper for working out the math

That's it, and that's all you're permitted to use. No outside notes, formula sheets, or references.

## If you need an accommodation, ask early

If you have a disability or condition that affects how you take the test, request an accommodation with your local JATC *before* you're scheduled. These arrangements can't be set up on test day itself, so the time to raise it is during the application process, not when you walk in.

## After the test

You won't get your score on the spot. Most locals take somewhere between two and four weeks to receive results and let you know whether you qualified. It's a bit of a wait, which is a good reason to walk in confident rather than hoping.
