# Failed the IBEW Aptitude Test? Here's What to Do Next

**Short answer: not qualifying isn't the end of the road. You'll wait 90 days at most locals, six months at some, before you can retake it. That time is more than enough to fix what went wrong, as long as you find out what actually went wrong. Here's how to make the next attempt count.**

First, take a breath. Not getting a qualifying score feels like a door slamming, especially when this is the career you're aiming for. It isn't. Plenty of people who are journeymen today didn't qualify on their first try. The test is a checkpoint, not a verdict on whether you belong in the trade.

Here's how to turn the setback into a better second attempt.

## What happens now

If you didn't hit the qualifying score (usually a [4 out of 9](/blog/good-score-ibew-aptitude-test)), you don't move to the interview this cycle, and there's a mandatory waiting period before you can test again. At most locals that's now 90 days; some still require six months, and the clock follows you nationally, so you can't just test at a different local next week.

The full breakdown, including why the number varies and the one thing that can get you disqualified, is here: [how long you have to wait to retake the test](/blog/how-long-to-retake-ibew-aptitude-test). Read it before you do anything else, because retaking too early can cost you far more than a few months.

## Why "study harder" usually doesn't work

Here's the mistake that traps most people on the second attempt. They spend the waiting period studying more in general, walk back in, and score about the same. Not because they didn't work, but because they spread that work evenly across everything instead of aiming it at the specific things that cost them the first time.

A score below the cutoff is almost never "I'm bad at all of this." It's usually two or three specific weak spots, in the math or the reading, quietly dragging the whole number down. If you don't know which ones, you end up re-reviewing what you already knew and never touching the thing that actually failed you.

## How to come back stronger

The move is to diagnose before you study. Find out exactly which skills held you back, fix those in order, and leave the rest alone. Done that way, the wait stops feeling like punishment and turns into a clear, finite to-do list.
